Concept

Global Identity Postcard is a speculative design object and the first chapter of a narrative trilogy exploring cross-civilization communication, human cognition, and the limits of rationality.

This project began as a speculative design question: what conditions would actually produce a unified human identity? Not aspiration, not shared values, not diplomacy. The answer the project arrives at is threat. A civilization-level external threat is the only historical force that reliably dissolves the boundaries that otherwise define human groups against each other. A shared fear is the most effective path to a global identity.

Front Face — Alien Signal Encoding
The Object

The postcard is a physical artifact from a silicon-based civilization. It is small enough to hold, strange enough to keep. The front surface encodes an alien signal in a relief that resists easy interpretation. The back maps the structural relationship between three civilizations: the senders, humanity, and the implied third party whose presence makes the invitation also a warning.

The object is 3D printed in black, with a material texture that reads as both technological and ancient. The choice to make it a postcard — the most intimate scale of human correspondence — is deliberate. Something this consequential arriving in this form collapses the distance between the cosmic and the personal.

Signal Design

The two circular forms on the front face encode the alien signal using a visual language designed to be clearly non-human while remaining formally legible. The organic irregularity of the left form and the geometric precision of the right suggest two different modes of meaning — one biological, one mathematical — existing in the same transmission.

The alien script along the postcard's surface was designed as a complete typographic system, not a decorative pattern. Each character has a consistent internal logic, suggesting a functional written language rather than invented ornament.

Alien Typographic System
Technical Process

The object was designed in Rhino and 3D printed in black PLA. The relief depth on both faces was calibrated so that the forms read legibly under varied lighting conditions — deep enough to cast meaningful shadows, shallow enough to remain precise at postcard scale.

The Trilogy

This postcard is the inciting artifact of a three-part speculative narrative. In Part II, Thought Fortress, humanity attempts to decode the messenger sent in response to accepting the invitation — and fails systematically. In Part III, Flesh Override, the spacecraft departs for first contact and its navigation systems collapse, forcing the crew to navigate by ancient divination. The postcard is the moment before humanity decides how to respond. Everything that follows is consequence.
