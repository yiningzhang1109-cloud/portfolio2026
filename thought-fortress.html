Concept

Thought Fortress is an interactive narrative experience built in P5.js. Humanity has decided to respond to the alien invitation. Scientists are attempting to decode the mechanical messenger through pattern recognition experiments. The player takes the role of a researcher running these tests across three levels.

The three-level design systematically dismantles a different cognitive assumption at each stage. Each level is also a trap: the player thinks they are studying the entity. The entity may be studying them.

Level 1

Symmetry as Comfort

Bilateral symmetry matching human aesthetic preferences. The pattern feels familiar, almost elegant. The player gains confidence. The system is learning what makes humans feel in control.

Level 2

Symmetry Breaks

Crystal growth logic that violates symmetry expectations while remaining partially legible. The player can still find patterns, but the patterns no longer behave. Confidence begins to erode.

Level 3

Logic Overridden

Lines intersect. The geometric intuition that this cannot be right is exactly what must be abandoned. The player is being asked to accept spatial relationships that have no equivalent in human experience.

Crystal Growth as Alien Logic

The visual language of each level is drawn from real crystal growth patterns — a natural process that follows strict internal rules while producing forms that appear irregular or even chaotic to human perception. This was a deliberate design choice: the alien entity is not random. It has logic. Human researchers simply cannot parse that logic from the outside.

The hexagonal node structure references the symmetry axes of real crystal lattices, where three growth directions produce forms that appear to violate bilateral symmetry while obeying a deeper, non-human geometry.

The Evolution State

After the player completes all three levels, the system enters an autonomous expansion state. Nodes multiply without player input. The network grows past the edges of the screen. The player cannot stop it, cannot determine whether their research process triggered this response, or whether the loss of control was the predetermined outcome from the beginning.

That irresolvability is the piece's argument: human helplessness in the presence of genuinely advanced intelligence is not a failure of effort. It is a structural condition. The scientists in Clear River could not name what was wrong. The researcher in Thought Fortress cannot stop what they have started.

Technical Approach

Built entirely in P5.js. Each level is initialized as a graph data structure — nodes with coordinates and connection arrays — so the growth logic can be extended procedurally in the evolution state. The color palette shifts across levels: purple to green to gold, tracking the movement from familiar to alien. The dashed lines indicate target connections the player must complete, always pulling toward configurations that feel geometrically wrong.
